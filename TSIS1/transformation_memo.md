## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kaspi.kz is, by any consumer product standard, a world-class product-led organization. Its FY 2024 results — **$5.318B revenue (+32% YoY), 75.09% operating margin, 10.2M monthly active users** — reflect a company that has mastered outcome-oriented thinking for its customer-facing Super App. CEO Mikheil Lomtadze's founding philosophy of small, NPS-driven product teams has produced the dominant digital ecosystem in Kazakhstan.

However, this product excellence has a shadow: **Kaspi's internal functions — Legal, Compliance, and Procurement — operate in a fundamentally different model.** These departments remain trapped in an IT-Service mindset, functioning as cost centers that process requests rather than empowered teams solving business problems. This memo diagnoses that gap and proposes a path forward.

---

## GAP ANALYSIS AGAINST CAGAN'S PRODUCT OPERATING MODEL

### Pillar 1 — Empowered Product Teams ⚠️ PARTIAL PASS

Kaspi's consumer-facing teams are genuinely empowered. The 2024 Annual Report (SEC Form 20-F) confirms teams are "led by a directly responsible individual" with clear ownership. Marketplace GMV grew 39% YoY and e-commerce purchases 123% YoY — evidence of autonomous, fast-moving teams.

**THE GAP:** This empowerment does not extend to back-office functions. The Legal and Compliance department, which oversees Kaspi's obligations under Kazakhstan's Anti-Money Laundering Law, AFDRA regulations, and KYC/sanctions compliance, has **no dedicated product team**. Legal operates as a shared service that responds to tickets from the business. There is no "Legal Product Manager," no defined outcome that Legal owns, and no authority to invest in tools independently. Legal is a dependency, not a team.

### Pillar 2 — Outcome vs. Output Orientation ❌ FAIL (Internal Functions)

Kaspi consumer products measure NPS and Monthly Active Users. The 2024 report cites "73 monthly transactions per active consumer" as a performance metric — a usage outcome, not a feature count.

**THE GAP:** Legal and Compliance teams measure outputs: number of contracts reviewed, tickets closed, audits completed. There is no documented outcome that the Compliance team owns (e.g., "reduce regulatory fine risk by X%" or "reduce contract approval cycle from 12 days to 3 days"). With **$5.318B in revenue** flowing through a regulatory landscape supervised by three separate agencies (National Bank of Kazakhstan, AFDRA, Financial Monitoring Agency), the absence of outcome ownership in Compliance is a strategic liability.

### Pillar 3 — Continuous Discovery ❌ FAIL

Kaspi's product teams run continuous consumer discovery. However, there is **zero evidence in the 2024 Annual Report or ESG Report** of structured internal discovery processes in Procurement or Legal. These teams do not interview their internal customers (Finance, Operations, Business Units) to understand unmet needs. They wait to be asked.

This is the classic **"IT-service" trap** Cagan identifies: the team delivers what is requested, not what is needed. A Procurement team that never asks "What slows you down the most?" will never discover that the real bottleneck is not vendor selection — it's legal review.

### Pillar 4 — Missionary vs. Mercenary Culture ❌ FAIL (Back-Office)

Kaspi's consumer mission — "to improve the lives of Kazakhstanis through technology" — is genuine and lived in the product org. Co-founders Kim and Lomtadze have instilled a missionary culture that produced 40% YoY growth in Payments transactions.

**THE GAP:** Back-office teams exhibit **mercenary dynamics**:
- Legal reviews contracts to avoid liability, not to enable faster business.
- Procurement processes vendors to close POs, not to reduce total cost of ownership.
- Performance is measured by throughput, not by business impact.

This is the "feature factory" equivalent for support functions: optimizing for activity, not outcomes.

---

## TOP 3 RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Appoint an Internal Products Lead.** Create a role responsible for internal tooling as product — not IT. This person owns the outcome of "internal team productivity" and runs discovery with Legal, Compliance, and Procurement.

2. **Define Outcome Metrics for Legal/Compliance.** Replace "contracts reviewed per week" with "average contract approval cycle time" and "regulatory finding rate." Connect these to business impact (deal velocity, revenue at risk).

3. **Launch a Legal Intelligence AI Pilot.** Given Kaspi's $200M+ annual technology investment and existing AWS infrastructure, the marginal cost of piloting an AI-assisted contract review agent is low. The first step is discovery — not deployment.
